Vitamins performs hundreds of roles in our body by converting the food into energy.

Things you need to know about Vitamin A

Vitamin A is also known as Retinol. It stimulates the production of white blood cells in our body and also helps in maintaining the healthy cells and thier growth. Moreover it is also vital for cell recognition, vision, immune function, and reproduction.



Benefits of Vitamin A

Vitamin A performs various functions in our body and helps in many ways.

  • It cures night blindness
  • It helps to treat dry skin
  • It lower Cancer risks
  • It makes skin and hair healthful
  • helps your body to defence against illness

Sources of Vitamin A

  • cheese
  • eggs
  • carrots
  • pumpkins
  • apricots
  • mango
  • oily fish
  • milk
  • liver products and meats

Intake values of Vitamin A

  • upto 3 years: 600 microgram(mcg) per day
  • 4 to 8 years: 900 microgram(mcg) per day
  • 9 to 13 years: 1,700 microgram(mcg) per day
  • 14 to 19 years: 2,800 microgram(mcg) per day
  • 19+ years: 3,000 microgram(mcg) per day

Risk of over consuming

The use of the topical treatment may also increase vitamin A to an unhealthy level.Mostly the teenagers uses vaious products of high retinol for their skins and other similar products which can increase the level of vitamin a to uncertainity that leads to skin damage.

However, people should avoid using them during pregnancy. The highest risk of overconsumption is with supplements. A healthful, balanced diet is unlikely to lead to toxic levels of vitamin A. It should also provide enough vitamin A without needing supplements.
